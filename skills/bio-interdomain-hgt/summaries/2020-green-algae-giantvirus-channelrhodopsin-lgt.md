# Lateral transfer of anion channelrhodopsins between green algae and giant viruses (2020)

**Venue**: bioRxiv preprint (2020.04.15.042127); subsequently published

## Key points
- Anion-conducting channelrhodopsins are shared between green algae (Chlorophyta)
  and giant viruses via lateral gene transfer — a concrete, functionally important
  green-algae <-> giant-virus exchange.
- Demonstrates that ecologically/functionally significant genes (here, light-gated
  ion channels) move across the host-virus boundary.

## Relevance to this skill
- Direct precedent when a query NCLDV's host-derived genes point to a green-algal
  (Chlorophyta) host; example of validating a transfer by gene-tree topology and
  shared functional signature rather than similarity alone.
- Chlorophyte-infecting chloroviruses/prasinoviruses are a useful comparison set.
