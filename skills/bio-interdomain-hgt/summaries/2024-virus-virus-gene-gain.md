# Gene transfer among viruses substantially contributes to gene gain of giant viruses (2024)

**Journal**: Molecular Biology and Evolution
**DOI**: 10.1093/molbev/msae161

## Key points
- A large share of giant-virus gene gain comes from virus-to-virus transfer, not
  only from cellular hosts.
- Apparent "host-derived" viral genes can in fact be virus<->virus acquisitions where
  another virus originally obtained the gene from a cell.

## Relevance to this skill
- Names the key confounder for host->virus calls. Mitigation (Step 7): build per-gene
  trees that include NCLDV + other-virus (phage, virophage, mirus) + cellular homologs,
  and require the focal gene to nest inside the EXPECTED clade. A focal that groups
  with other viruses rather than the host indicates virus<->virus, not host<->virus.
