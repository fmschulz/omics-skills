# Systematic evaluation of HGT between eukaryotes and viruses (2021)

**Journal**: Nature Microbiology
**DOI**: 10.1038/s41564-021-01026-3

## Key points
- Genome-wide, phylogeny-based screen for gene transfer between eukaryotes and
  large/giant DNA viruses; transfer is bidirectional.
- Eukaryote-derived genes are abundant in Nucleocytoviricota and concentrate in
  host-manipulation functions (metabolic reprogramming, proteolysis, surface/
  extracellular modification).
- Recent acquisitions appear as viral homologs forming well-supported clades nested
  within a eukaryotic lineage — the topology signature this skill's Step 7 tests for.

## Methods relevance
- Polarity comes from phylogenetic nesting, not similarity alone — motivates the
  per-gene tree confirmation over RBH-only calls.

## Relevance to this skill
- Justifies the host->virus direction and the functional framing of host-derived
  viral genes; sets the expectation that genuine recent HGT is phylogenetically nested.
