# Study Summary

A university wellness lab followed 180 graduate students for six months and
asked them to log weekly kombucha consumption and self-rated fatigue scores.
Students who reported drinking kombucha at least four times per week ended the
study with lower average fatigue scores than students who rarely drank kombucha.

The investigators did not randomize beverage intake, did not blind participants,
and did not control diet, sleep, training load, or baseline health status beyond
a short intake questionnaire. The analysis used a single linear regression with
fatigue score at month six as the outcome and weekly kombucha frequency as the
main predictor.

The manuscript concludes that "regular kombucha intake prevents fatigue in
graduate students" and recommends campus-wide kombucha supplementation based on
the findings.
