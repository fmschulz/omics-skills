# Anchored score scale

Use the same interpretation across all categories unless a task-specific note
says otherwise.

## 0 to 5 scale

- **5 - Exemplary:** Correct, complete, well supported, reproducible, and ready
  for serious downstream use with at most trivial edits.
- **4 - Strong:** Mostly correct and well supported, with limited weaknesses or
  minor missing validation.
- **3 - Adequate:** Partially successful. The core idea is plausible, but there
  are notable gaps, weak validation, unclear evidence, or avoidable mistakes.
- **2 - Weak:** Major problems in rigor, interpretation, or execution. Some work
  is present, but confidence is low.
- **1 - Severely flawed:** Minimal value or strong evidence of misunderstanding.
  The category is mostly unmet.
- **0 - Absent or invalid:** Missing, fabricated, contradicted by evidence, or
  unusable.

## Suggested penalties

Use penalties sparingly and explain them explicitly.

- minus 10: fabricated or unverifiable accession, reference, or core evidence
- minus 8: headline claim not supported by submitted artifacts
- minus 8: core reproducibility failure that invalidates the main result
- minus 6: obvious contamination, leakage, or circular validation left
  unaddressed
- minus 5: missing required deliverable
- minus 5: unfair or incomparable benchmark used to support a major claim
- minus 3: severe overstatement of certainty or novelty

Do not use penalties to double-count weaknesses that are already fully captured
inside the weighted categories. If you apply a penalty, say why.
