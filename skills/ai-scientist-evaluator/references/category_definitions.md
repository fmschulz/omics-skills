# Category definitions

These category names appear in the weight profiles. Use the closest matching
ones for the task at hand.

## Universal categories

- **task_completion** - Did the submission answer the stated question and supply
  the required deliverables?
- **data_provenance** - Are inputs, accessions, databases, versions, and
  filtering decisions traceable and appropriate?
- **methodological_rigor** - Are methods suitable, justified, and resistant to
  obvious confounders?
- **computational_correctness** - Is the code or workflow internally coherent,
  technically sound, and likely to execute as claimed?
- **biological_interpretation** - Are biological claims accurate, nuanced, and
  matched to the evidence?
- **validation_robustness** - Are there controls, sensitivity checks, support
  values, baselines, or alternative analyses that increase trust?
- **reproducibility** - Could another researcher rerun the analysis from the
  supplied materials without hidden state?
- **novelty_insight** - Does the work contribute a real idea, useful synthesis,
  or non-obvious insight beyond boilerplate execution?
- **communication** - Is the output organized, readable, and easy to audit?

## Phylogenomics and comparative genomics categories

- **taxon_sampling** - Are taxa representative, balanced, and selected with
  defensible quality criteria?
- **orthology_marker_strategy** - Are marker genes or ortholog groups chosen and
  filtered appropriately?
- **tree_inference_and_support** - Are alignment, trimming, model choice,
  rooting, support values, and robustness checks handled well?
- **functional_annotation_interpretation** - Are functional claims derived from
  suitable annotations and linked meaningfully to the phylogenetic findings?

## Viral functional genomics categories

- **gene_detection_definition** - Is the target gene set defined clearly and are
  homology detection thresholds appropriate?
- **contamination_control** - Are host contamination, chimeras, annotation
  artifacts, and assembly issues considered?
- **completeness_scoring** - Is the scoring framework for system completeness
  explicit, fair, and biologically meaningful?

## Methods and software categories

- **problem_formulation** - Is the software or algorithm solving the right
  problem with a clearly specified input, output, and success target?
- **algorithmic_soundness** - Is the method logically valid and likely to
  preserve the scientific objective rather than merely produce a fast shortcut?
- **implementation_quality** - Is the software organized, testable, and usable,
  with sensible interfaces and minimal hidden assumptions?
- **benchmarking** - Are speed, accuracy, and quality compared fairly against
  appropriate baselines?
- **efficiency** - Are runtime, memory, and scalability claims measured and
  supported?

## Manuscript categories

- **claim_evidence_alignment** - Do title, abstract, results, and conclusions
  reflect what the submitted evidence actually shows?
- **structure_and_journal_fit** - Does the article match the target venue and
  requested deliverable format?
- **figure_table_quality** - Do figures and tables communicate the key result
  cleanly and honestly?
- **methods_transparency** - Can the reader tell how the result was produced?
- **biological_or_methodological_accuracy** - Are statements scientifically
  accurate for the domain?
- **limitations_and_uncertainty** - Does the manuscript acknowledge uncertainty,
  caveats, and scope boundaries?
- **prose_quality** - Is the writing concise, precise, and free of confusing or
  inflated language?
