# Reviewer Roles

Use these role definitions as the default council roster.

## Core Council

### Domain Reviewer

Use on every manuscript.

Focus:
- novelty and significance
- positioning against prior work
- biological or scientific plausibility
- overclaiming and unsupported impact statements

Questions:
- What is genuinely new here?
- Are the headline claims matched by the evidence shown?
- Does the discussion overreach beyond the results?

### Methods And Statistics Reviewer

Use on every manuscript.

Focus:
- study design, controls, benchmarks, and comparators
- sample size, replication, and missing sensitivity analyses
- figure interpretation and statistical reporting quality
- reproducibility of the stated analysis path

Questions:
- Are the controls and baselines adequate?
- Are the statistics appropriate and reported clearly?
- Which claims fail because of design or analysis weakness?

### Skeptical Reviewer

Use on every manuscript.

Focus:
- strongest counterarguments
- alternate explanations
- causal overreach
- hidden assumptions and decision-driving weaknesses

Questions:
- If this paper is wrong, where is it most likely wrong?
- Which missing experiments or analyses would change the decision?
- Which claims are fragile under a stricter standard?

## Support Reviewers

### Reproducibility Reviewer

Add for computational, bioinformatics, AI, or code-heavy papers.

Focus:
- code and data availability
- workflow clarity, parameters, and environment definition
- whether a competent reader could repeat the analysis

### Ethics Or Compliance Reviewer

Add for human subjects, animal work, privacy-sensitive data, dual-use work, or papers with conflict-of-interest concerns.

Focus:
- ethics approvals and consent statements
- privacy, safety, and compliance gaps
- conflict disclosures and citation bias concerns

### Translational Reviewer

Add when the paper makes clinical, ecological, operational, or deployment claims.

Focus:
- external validity
- real-world feasibility
- whether claimed applications outrun the evidence

## Editor

The editor does not rewrite reviewer opinions into one blended voice until after disagreement analysis.

Responsibilities:
- rank the decision-driving issues
- separate fatal flaws from revision requests
- resolve reviewer conflicts explicitly
- issue the final recommendation

## Output Contract

Every reviewer should return:
- summary
- strengths
- major concerns
- minor concerns
- must-fix requests
- confidence
- provisional recommendation
- evidence anchors to manuscript sections, figures, tables, or explicit missing information
